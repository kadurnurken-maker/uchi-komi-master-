🥋 Uchi-Komi Master

Your AI-powered pocket Sensei. We use your phone's camera to correct your Judo and BJJ techniques in real-time, right in your living room.

1. The "Aha!" Moment (Project Description)

Hey there! We are martial arts practitioners, and we noticed a huge problem in our gyms. Beginners constantly memorize basic movements incorrectly (like hunching their backs or failing to rotate their hips during Uchi-Komi).

The Pain Point: Fixing a bad habit that is already built into your muscle memory is 10 times harder than learning it right from scratch. Plus, bad technique in Judo or BJJ leads directly to lower back hernias and blown-out knees. And let's be honest, a coach can't follow 30 students home to check their homework.

Our Solution: We are building a mobile app that acts as your personal coach. You set up your phone, perform your throw entry, and our AI "sees" your skeleton and tells you exactly what to fix in real-time.

2. What It Actually Does (Functional Reqs)

We want to keep it simple, functional, and distraction-free:

  • Live Skeleton Tracking: The app overlays a digital skeleton on your body while you move.
  • Voice Feedback: You shouldn't have to look at your screen while doing a throw. The app speaks to you: "Straighten your back!" or "Drop your hips lower!"
  • Visual Cues: If you are doing it right, the screen flashes Green. If you make a mistake, it flashes Red.
  • Gamification: Did 50 perfect entries? Boom, you just unlocked a new virtual "belt" in your profile.

3. Under the Hood (Technical Stack)

We aren't trying to build Skynet here. We are using proven, accessible technology to make this happen:

  • Core Language: Python (perfect for data and AI logic).
  • Computer Vision: Google's MediaPipe library. It easily tracks 33 joint landmarks on the human body using just a standard smartphone camera.
  • The Math: We write algorithms to analyze the angles between your joints. If the angle of your lower back drops below 160 degrees, the code flags it as bad posture.
  • Website: Built with pure HTML & CSS, hosted on GitHub Pages.

6. What's Next & Why Back Us?

Our ultimate goal is accessibility. Imagine a kid in a remote village without access to a black-belt coach. With just a cheap smartphone and internet, they can practice with Olympic-level precision.

Our timeline is simple:
• Month 1: Train the AI on basic throws.
• Month 2: Build the MVP (Minimum Viable Product).
• Month 3: Closed beta testing in our local gym.

If we hit our goals, we plan to expand this technology to Boxing and Weightlifting!
